# Case 01: Missing Arbitration Institution

## Facts

Company A and Company B sign a contract containing the clause:

> “Any dispute arising from this contract shall be resolved by arbitration.”

A dispute later arises.

## Issue

Is the arbitration clause valid if no arbitration institution is specified?

## Analysis

Under Chinese arbitration law, an arbitration agreement must clearly identify an arbitration commission or provide a method to determine one.

In this case:

* The clause shows intention to arbitrate
* However, no arbitration institution is named
* No method is provided to select one

This creates uncertainty in enforcement.

## Conclusion

The clause is likely invalid or unenforceable due to failure to specify an arbitration institution.

## Exam Tip

Always check:

* Is the arbitration institution clearly identified?
* If not, is there a valid method to determine it?
