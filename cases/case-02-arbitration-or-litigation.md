# Case 02: Arbitration or Litigation Option Clause

## Facts

A contract states:

> “The parties may resolve disputes by arbitration or litigation.”

## Issue

Is this arbitration clause valid?

## Analysis

A valid arbitration agreement must show a clear and exclusive intention to arbitrate.

In this case:

* The clause gives two options: arbitration OR litigation
* It does not require arbitration exclusively
* The intention to arbitrate is unclear

## Conclusion

The clause is generally considered invalid because it lacks a clear commitment to arbitration.

## Exam Tip

If the clause allows both court and arbitration, it usually fails the validity test.
