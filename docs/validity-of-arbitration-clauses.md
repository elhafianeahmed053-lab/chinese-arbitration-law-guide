# Chapter 4: Validity of Arbitration Clauses  
### Chinese Arbitration Law – Academic Guide

---

## 1. Introduction

The validity of an arbitration clause determines whether disputes can be resolved through arbitration.

If a clause is invalid, courts will generally assume jurisdiction.

---

## 2. Requirements for Validity

A valid arbitration clause must include:

- Clear intention to arbitrate  
- Defined scope of disputes  
- Identifiable arbitration institution  

---

## 3. Valid Clause Example

> Any dispute arising from this contract shall be submitted to CIETAC for arbitration.

This clause is valid because all legal requirements are met.

---

## 4. Invalid Clause Examples

### 4.1 Unclear intention

> Parties may choose arbitration or litigation.

❌ Invalid because intention is unclear.

---

### 4.2 No institution specified

> Disputes shall be resolved by arbitration.

❌ Invalid because no arbitration institution is identified.

---

## 5. Legal Effect of Invalid Clause

If invalid:

- Courts may take jurisdiction  
- Arbitration cannot proceed  

---

## 6. Conclusion

Validity is the first step in any arbitration analysis. Without validity, jurisdiction cannot be established.
