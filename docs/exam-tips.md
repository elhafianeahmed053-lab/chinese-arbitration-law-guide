# Exam Tips

## Introduction

Questions on Chinese arbitration law often focus on arbitration agreements, jurisdiction, and the validity of arbitration clauses. A structured approach can help students answer these questions clearly and efficiently.

## Five-Step Method

When analyzing an arbitration problem:

### Step 1: Identify the Arbitration Clause

Locate the clause in the contract and determine its exact wording.

### Step 2: Examine Validity

Ask:

* Is there a clear intention to arbitrate?
* Is the scope of disputes identified?
* Is the arbitration institution identifiable?

### Step 3: Determine Jurisdiction

If the arbitration agreement is valid, the arbitral tribunal will generally have jurisdiction.

If the agreement is invalid, the dispute may be heard by a court.

### Step 4: Apply the Law to the Facts

Explain how the legal requirements relate to the facts of the problem.

### Step 5: Reach a Conclusion

Provide a clear and direct answer.

## Quick Validity Checklist

Before concluding that an arbitration clause is valid, confirm:

✓ Written agreement

✓ Clear intention to arbitrate

✓ Scope of disputes identified

✓ Arbitration institution identifiable

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1

Ignoring the validity of the arbitration clause.

Always determine validity before discussing jurisdiction.

### Mistake 2

Failing to identify the arbitration institution.

The identity of the arbitration institution is often decisive.

### Mistake 3

Providing conclusions without legal analysis.

Support every conclusion with legal reasoning.

## Sample Exam Question

### Question

A contract states:

> Any dispute arising from this contract shall be submitted to CIETAC for arbitration.

Is the clause valid?

### Answer

Yes. The clause demonstrates a clear intention to arbitrate, defines the scope of disputes, and identifies the arbitration institution. Therefore, it will generally be considered valid.

## Final Advice

Read the facts carefully, identify the arbitration clause, analyze validity first, then discuss jurisdiction. A logical structure often earns more marks than a lengthy answer.
